About Me

I grew up in Taiwan and bootstrapped my way to US at 20. By "bootstrapping," I mean there was never a predefined roadmap or checklist to follow. I see my journey as a jump diffusion, driven by curiosity, a pursuit of impact, and a fear of mediocrity.

Inside science-fiction films and games, I developed a fascination with "simulation" long before formal study, which eventually led me to study physics in a selective national high school science program. Beyond this path, however, I also founded (perhaps "faked" in the Silicon Valley sense) a national representative team for the European Physics Olympiad, where I won medals and earned early, test-free admission to National Taiwan University.

The year I entered college, ChatGPT emerged and prompted me to shift from physics to AI/ML research. I was fortunate to work with Northwestern University (MAGICS Lab), Gamma Paradigm Capital, and Caltech (AI4Science Lab). Through research, I attended top conferences and met some of the most brilliant minds on Earth. It became apparent that what truly mattered to me was finding a path to the US. So I left NTU, applied for transfer, and made it to the University of Washington.

My ultimate vision is to create an "experience machine" that empowers every individual with the right to choose an ego-centric, reversible, and eternal second life. My contrarian view is that, though often seen as degenerate or dystopian, it may be the most humanized act compared to eras of slavery and labor, in terms of freedom and creativity. Macroscopically, it is also the most efficient means, at least within silicon-based technology, to mine human desire into realized utility.
